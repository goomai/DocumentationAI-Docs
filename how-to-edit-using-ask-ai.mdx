# How to Edit Content Using Ask AI

## Improve and Refine Your Writing with AI Assistance

The Ask AI editing feature helps you improve existing content by highlighting text and applying AI-powered refinements. Perfect for polishing drafts, improving clarity, adjusting tone, and ensuring your writing meets professional standards.

---

## When to Use Ask AI for Editing

**Perfect for:**
- Improving clarity and readability
- Adjusting tone and style
- Fixing grammar and structure issues
- Making content more concise or detailed
- Restructuring paragraphs for better flow
- Adapting content for different audiences

---

## Step-by-Step Content Editing

### **Step 1: Select Your Text**
**Highlight the section** you want to edit. This can be:
- A single sentence
- Multiple sentences
- An entire paragraph
- Several paragraphs

### **Step 2: Access Ask AI**
With your text selected, click the ✨ **Ask AI** button or press **Ctrl+J**. The AI menu will appear with editing-specific options.

### **Step 3: Choose Your Editing Action**
**Use Predefined Commands:**
Select from common editing tasks like:
- "Improve clarity"
- "Make more concise" 
- "Expand with details"
- "Fix grammar"
- "Change tone"
- "Restructure for flow"

**Use Custom Prompts:**
Enter specific editing instructions such as:
- "Make this more formal for academic writing"
- "Simplify this for a general audience"
- "Add more specific examples"
- "Rewrite in active voice"

### **Step 4: Apply Changes**
Review the AI's suggested improvements and accept the changes that enhance your content. You can always undo changes if needed.

<Callout kind="success" collapsed="false">
Editing with Ask AI maintains your original meaning while improving how your ideas are expressed and presented.
</Callout>

## Types of Editing You Can Do

### **Style and Tone Adjustments**
| Original Issue | Ask AI Command | Result |
|----------------|----------------|---------|
| Too informal for academic paper | "Make this more formal" | Professional, scholarly tone |
| Too complex for general audience | "Simplify this language" | Clear, accessible writing |
| Passive voice overuse | "Rewrite in active voice" | Stronger, more direct sentences |

### **Structure and Flow Improvements**
- **Paragraph Organization:** "Restructure this paragraph for better flow"
- **Sentence Variety:** "Vary the sentence structure in this section"
- **Logical Sequence:** "Reorder these points logically"
- **Transition Improvement:** "Add better transitions between these ideas"

### **Content Enhancement**
- **Add Detail:** "Expand this with more specific examples"
- **Reduce Redundancy:** "Remove repetitive information"
- **Strengthen Arguments:** "Make this argument more convincing"
- **Improve Evidence:** "Add supporting details to this claim"

## Common Editing Commands

### **Clarity and Readability**
- **"Improve clarity"** - Makes complex sentences easier to understand
- **"Simplify this language"** - Reduces jargon and complex terminology
- **"Make this more concise"** - Removes unnecessary words and redundancy
- **"Expand with details"** - Adds relevant information and examples

### **Grammar and Style**
- **"Fix grammar"** - Corrects grammatical errors and awkward phrasing
- **"Improve sentence structure"** - Enhances flow and readability
- **"Use active voice"** - Converts passive constructions to active
- **"Vary sentence length"** - Creates more dynamic, engaging prose

### **Tone and Audience**
- **"Make more formal"** - Adjusts for academic or professional contexts
- **"Make more conversational"** - Creates friendly, approachable tone
- **"Adjust for [specific audience]"** - Tailors content for target readers
- **"Match the tone of the rest of the document"** - Maintains consistency

## Advanced Editing Techniques

### **Targeted Improvements**
Instead of general commands, be specific about what you want to improve:
- "Make this introduction more engaging"
- "Strengthen the conclusion with a call to action"
- "Add credibility to this claim with evidence"
- "Make this explanation clearer for beginners"

### **Comparative Editing**
Ask for multiple options to choose from:
- "Rewrite this sentence three different ways"
- "Show me formal and informal versions of this paragraph"
- "Give me shorter and longer versions of this explanation"

### **Style Matching**
Use the document context to maintain consistency:
- "Match the writing style of the previous paragraph"
- "Adjust the tone to be consistent with the introduction"
- "Rewrite using the same level of formality as the rest of the document"

---

## Best Practices for Effective Editing

### **Start with Clear Selection**
Make sure you've selected the exact text you want to edit. Selecting too much or too little can affect the quality of AI suggestions.

### **Be Specific with Instructions**
Instead of "make this better," use specific commands like "make this more persuasive" or "reduce the word count by half."

### **Consider Context**
The AI considers the surrounding content, so edits will maintain consistency with your overall document style and flow.

### **Review Changes Carefully**
Always read through AI-suggested edits to ensure they maintain your intended meaning and improve the text as desired.

## Example Editing Scenarios

### **Academic Writing**
**Before:** "There are a lot of studies that show social media can be bad for mental health."
**Command:** "Make this more formal with specific evidence"
**After:** "Multiple peer-reviewed studies demonstrate a correlation between excessive social media use and increased rates of anxiety and depression among adolescents."

### **Business Writing**
**Before:** "Our sales were really good this quarter and we think they'll keep being good."
**Command:** "Make this more professional with specific details"
**After:** "Q3 sales exceeded targets by 15%, and current market indicators suggest continued growth potential through Q4."

### **General Content**
**Before:** "The thing about renewable energy is that it's important because fossil fuels are running out and also bad for the environment."
**Command:** "Improve clarity and structure"
**After:** "Renewable energy addresses two critical challenges: the finite nature of fossil fuel reserves and their environmental impact on climate change."

## Troubleshooting Editing Issues

### **Edits change my meaning**
**Solution:** Be more specific about what aspects to change. For example, "improve grammar but keep the original meaning" or "only fix sentence structure."

### **Style doesn't match the rest of my document**
**Solution:** Include context in your prompt: "Edit this to match the tone of my introduction" or "keep the same level of formality as the previous section."

### **Edits are too conservative or too aggressive**
**Solution:** Specify the level of change you want: "minor grammar fixes only" or "completely rewrite for better flow."

### **Technical terms get removed inappropriately**
**Solution:** Specify what to preserve: "improve clarity but keep all technical terminology" or "simplify without removing industry-specific terms."

---

## Editing Workflow Tips

### **Progressive Refinement**
1. First pass: Focus on major structural and flow issues
2. Second pass: Address clarity and readability
3. Final pass: Polish grammar, style, and minor details

### **Section-by-Section Approach**
Edit smaller sections rather than entire documents for more focused, effective improvements.

### **Save Versions**
Keep track of different versions so you can compare changes and revert if needed.

<Callout kind="note" collapsed="false">
The most effective editing happens when you use Ask AI to enhance your own editorial judgment, not replace it. Use the AI's suggestions as a starting point for further refinement.
</Callout>
