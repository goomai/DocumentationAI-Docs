## What Are Projects?

Projects are organizational containers that group your content by brand, website, or business entity. Each project stores important information about your brand, domain, target audience, and content strategy, allowing our AI tools to create more targeted and effective content.

A **Project** is a brand/site space (e.g., `paperguide.ai`). It stores brand context the agent uses while generating articles and selecting keywords, so it can accurately reference your **features, products, and terminology** when it’s relevant to the article type.

**Better SEO Optimization**

- AI understands your domain authority and competition level

- Content is optimized for your specific website's SEO needs

- Keyword suggestions are tailored to your niche and authority

**Brand Consistency**

- Maintains consistent tone and style across all content

- Incorporates your brand voice and terminology

- Ensures messaging aligns with your brand positioning

**Improved Content Quality**

- AI creates content that fits your audience and industry

## Creating Your First Project

### **Step 1: Access Project Creation**

You can create a new project in two ways:

- **From any tool**: Look for the **+ Project** option in the project dropdown menu

- **From project settings**: Navigate to your account settings and find the projects section

When you select **+ Project**, you'll see the "Create New Project" form with all the fields described below.

### **Step 2: Essential Project Information**

<Expandable uid="d16690e9-e6f4-496b-96c7-b89fd0f3a677" title="Required Project Fields" default-open="true">
  **Website URL**
  Enter your website's full URL (e.g., [https://yoursite.com](https://yoursite.com)). The AI uses this to:

  - Understand your domain authority and SEO context

  - Analyze your site's existing content and structure

  - Provide more accurate keyword suggestions

  - Optimize content for your specific website

  **Project Name**
  Create a descriptive name that includes your brand (e.g., "TechCorp: B2B SaaS Marketing Platform"). This helps you:

  - Quickly identify projects in dropdowns

  - Organize multiple brands or websites

  - Share context with team members

  **Brand Tone**
  Describe your brand's voice and communication style. Be specific about how you want to sound.

  *Good examples:*

  - "Professional yet approachable, with a focus on expertise and innovation"

  - "Friendly and conversational, like talking to a knowledgeable friend"

  - "Authoritative and technical, but accessible to non-experts"

  **About Project**
  Briefly describe your brand and core products or services. The AI references this when generating articles to:

  - Include relevant product mentions naturally

  - Create accurate company descriptions

  - Provide context-appropriate examples

  - Ensure content aligns with your business model

  *Example:* "Paperguide is an all-in-one AI Research Assistant to get research-backed answers, find & analyse research papers, manage references, and write documents"

  **Your Brand Terms**
  List specific words, phrases, product names, taglines, or acronyms you want the AI to use. Include:

  - Product names and features

  - Industry-specific terminology

  - Branded processes or methodologies

  - Important acronyms or technical terms

  - Taglines or key messaging phrases

  *Examples:* "paper guide", "essay title generator", "research paper writer", "citation manager"
</Expandable>

### **Step 3: Save and Test**

1. Review all information for accuracy

2. Click **Save** to create your project

3. Test by creating a sample article to see how project context improves results

<Callout uid="96902b91-1cd2-48d2-a549-336695521df8" kind="success" collapsed="false">
  The more detailed and specific your project information, the better the AI can create content that sounds like it was written by someone who truly understands your business.
</Callout>

## Best Practices for Project Setup

### **Website URL Tips**

- Use your main domain URL including https\://

- For blogs on subdomains, use the specific subdomain (e.g., blog.yoursite.com)

- If you have multiple related sites, create separate projects for each

### **Project Naming Best Practices**

Include your brand name and purpose for easy identification:

<Callout uid="f74bf9b9-9b77-44ab-a1ca-030d3efaa784" kind="success" collapsed="false">
  **Good Examples:** "Paperguide: AI Research Assistant", "TechCorp: B2B Marketing Blog", "EcomStore: Product Content"
</Callout>

### **Writing Effective Brand Tone Descriptions**

Be specific about how you want to sound. Avoid generic terms:

**Instead of:** "Professional and friendly"\
**Try:** "Professional yet approachable, like an expert consultant explaining complex topics to busy executives"

**Instead of:** "Casual"\
**Try:** "Conversational and relatable, like talking to a knowledgeable friend who keeps things simple"

### **About Project Guidelines**

Keep it concise but informative. Focus on:

- What your company does (core function)

- Who you serve (target audience)

- Key products or services

- Your unique value proposition

**Example structure:** "\[Company] is a \[type of solution] that helps \[target audience] to \[main benefits/outcomes]"

### **Brand Terms Strategy**

Include 8-15 of your most important terms:

- **Product names**: Your actual product names and features

- **Industry terms**: Specific terminology your audience uses

- **Key phrases**: Important concepts or processes you're known for

- **Acronyms**: Any abbreviations specific to your business

- **Competitive terms**: Alternative names people might search for

<Callout uid="e7ce8e20-69e4-45d9-8a85-4fa2e6a9872c" kind="note" collapsed="false">
  The AI uses these terms naturally throughout your content, so include variations of how people might search for your products.
</Callout>

## Project Settings You Can Update

### **Anytime Updates**

- **Brand Tone**: Refine your voice description as your brand evolves

- **About Project**: Update product descriptions, add new services, or revise positioning

- **Brand Terms**: Add new product names, remove outdated terms, or include seasonal keywords

- **Project Name**: Change for better organization or rebranding

### **Use With Caution**

- **Website URL**: Changing this affects SEO analysis and keyword suggestions for existing content

<Expandable uid="2837a2d1-8752-4bdd-87c9-84068fa7e707" title="How to Update Project Settings" default-open="false">
  1. Go to your project dropdown and find your project

  2. Look for an **Edit** or **Settings** option (usually near the project name)

  3. Make necessary changes to any field

  4. Click **Save** to apply changes

  5. New content will use updated settings immediately

  **Note:** Existing content isn't automatically updated when you change project settings. Consider regenerating important articles if you make major brand or product changes.
</Expandable>

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### **"I can't find my project in the dropdown"**

**Solutions:**

- Refresh the page and try again

- Check if you're logged into the correct account

- Verify the project wasn't accidentally deleted

- Contact support if the issue persists

### **"Content doesn't seem to use my project settings"**

**Solutions:**

- Confirm you selected the correct project before creating content

- Check that project settings are completely filled out

- Try creating a test article to see if settings are working

- Update project settings if they seem outdated

### **"Should I create separate projects for different content types?"**

**Answer:** Usually not necessary. One project per brand/website works best. Only create separate projects if you have genuinely different:

- Target audiences

- Brand voices

- Competitive landscapes

- Business goals
